Goal: Productivity
  • Let's go back to basics- get your Stitch Fix styling-only area ready and set your schedule for the time you feel your best self
  • Plan ahead: carve out blocks of styling time. A 2 hour "groove" is highly recommended so you can get in the styling zone without distractions

Goal: Balancing quality and efficiency
  • Use a timer: Break down your styling to three zones of
    1. Familiarizing yourself with client's history/Pinterest/requests (~2 mins)
    2. Pulling pieces
    3. Note writing
  • Each Fix should take about 12 minutes from start to finish - see which areas you can improve your timing on.
Styling tips that work
  • Anchor pieces: start with a bottom or dress as your jumping off point
  • Hone in on your vision for the Fix after choosing your anchor - stick to it and don't go back to scrolling!
  • Choose high match scores (over 40%, preferably)
  • Use advanced filters to add variety to your client's closet and match their preferences
  • Keep an open mind and be confident - trust your creative styling prowess.

Strategies that don't work
  • "Burst styling" styling and then writing notes later - data shows it causes lower AOV and fph
  • <5 minute styling - data shows this leads to low client satisfaction
  • Holding Fixes in your queue in hopes of a certain piece - trust the algorithm!
  • Working off the clock - we want you to be paid for your time and never want you working off the clock

Remember our Client Promise
  • Every client should feel like their Fix is a personalized experience, giving them effortless style
  • Leave your personal style at the door - always address requests and preferences
  • Continue building a connection!
